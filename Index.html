An Untitled Short

A Short Film by Andrew Shenkman & Carol Burbank

Myazaki by-way-of Pintrest, this short is a dazzling journey through a hand-crafted fantasy world that celebrates creativity, ingenuity and drive. An unnamed girl travels down a mist shrouded river towards a fearsome castle that floats in the sky before utilizing fantastic machines of her own creation to scale the castle walls. To defeat a fearsome Harpy Queen that resides there, she must tap into a sublime power that cannot be understood or controlled. Will she survive to tell the tale?

Visual Effects

This film is an ambitious marriage of traditional and modern film-making. Avoiding digital effects, the filmmakers unite the traditional techniques of paper-craft and diorama making with green-screen technology to set their film in a fantastical universe.

The sets are large-scale dioramas constructed with accordion-style paper layers, to create depth and fantasy. The actors are shot on green-screen and, during editing, shrunk and added into the diorama, to interact with and move through the paper-craft world. The final effect is a seamless and otherworldly experience, where the imaginary exists.

As for our otherworldly characters - the satyr and harpy - makeup, props and costumes will bring these individuals to life.

About the Filmmakers

On any given Wednesday, Andrew and Carol can be found in Andrew's apartment listening to obscure records, googling images of half-human beings and talking loudly.

With an admiration for Myazaki, Samurai Jack, Tim Burton, and analogue filmmaking, Andrew and Carol's approach to filmmaking is take a difficult idea, make it impossible, then ask all their friends to do it for free.

Born with the same brand of weird, their creative projects move forward in spite of their short attention-spans because they are supremely optimistic.

This is their first film together.

Carol Burbank

Carol's life is a performance currently in act V, or perhaps at intermission.
